What We Build

Two complementary business tracks, one product standard.

OurAppsX operates under the ArcadeForge Studios identity with a product system designed around durable App Store economics. Utility apps and mobile games are run as separate tracks, but both follow the same operating principles: clarity of purpose, low-friction interaction, and monetization that respects user intent.

Utility Applications

We build practical iOS tools that remove friction from everyday tasks such as conversion, organization, cleanup, and content enhancement. Speed, local processing, and trust are treated as product features, not marketing claims.

  • Fast execution for high-frequency user jobs
  • Lean interfaces with near-zero onboarding burden
  • IAP-led monetization through subscriptions and premium unlocks

Mobile Games

We develop lightweight casual and hyper-casual experiences with clean loops, strong feedback, and rapid restarts. The objective is not content bloat; it is satisfying mechanics with excellent eCPM potential and healthy retention.

  • Short session design with immediate replay value
  • IAA-led monetization with rewarded and interstitial placements
  • Optional IAP bundles for deeper player conversion

Commercial Discipline

Every release is evaluated through a unified framework covering funnel behavior, store compliance, creative positioning, ad suitability, subscription conversion, and long-term maintainability across Apple devices.

  • Measured release sequencing and iterative testing
  • Privacy-aware analytics and consent-aware monetization
  • Operational readiness for App Store and Google Play policy adaptation
Monetization Architecture

IAA and IAP are not mixed casually. They are mapped to product behavior.

In utilities, premium conversion is built around value thresholds such as batch processing, export quality, advanced automation, or workflow acceleration. In games, revenue is structured around player-controlled value exchange through rewarded videos and carefully placed interstitials, supported by optional in-app purchases where intent is strong.

Common Questions

Clear answers for partners, publishers, and store-facing stakeholders.

Why split the business into utilities and games?

Because the user intent, monetization timing, lifecycle expectations, and performance goals differ materially. Utilities reward depth and repeat workflow value, while games reward immediate engagement and session-based ad opportunity. Treating them as separate tracks improves product clarity and commercial precision.

How do you approach ads without harming retention?

We treat ad delivery as part of experience design. Rewarded ads are opt-in and tied to obvious value. Interstitial frequency is controlled by context, session rhythm, and player fatigue signals. Unsuitable ad density is considered a product defect.

Can you support white-label, outsourced, or strategic builds?

Yes. The studio supports selective external development and strategic collaboration for Apple-platform products, especially where partners need strong execution around UX, monetization logic, store compliance, or post-launch iteration discipline.
